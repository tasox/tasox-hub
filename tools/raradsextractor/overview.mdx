Recent **WinRAR 0-day vulnerabilities** — including CVE-2025-8088 and CVE-2025-6218 — have highlighted how attackers abuse RAR archives in phishing campaigns.

Adversaries weaponize RAR archives by:
- Embedding **Alternate Data Streams (ADS)** inside files in the archive
- Using **malformed headers** or **XTRA blocks** to bypass detection
- Leveraging WinRAR parsing vulnerabilities to achieve **code execution** when archives are opened

Traditional tools like `unrar.exe` or WinRAR's GUI can be **dangerous to run** on malicious samples and may **not expose hidden ADS streams**.

<Info>
  **rarADSExtractor** provides a safe, Python-only parser for **Incident Responders, Threat Hunters, and SOC analysts**. [GitHub Repository](https://github.com/tasox/rarADSExtractor)
</Info>
